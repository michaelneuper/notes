#+title: Closed Interval Method
#+filetags: calculus derivatives

#+begin_quote
To find the [[id:8e80da2f-092f-476a-b5f8-08749dc9dc1e][Absolute maximum and minimum values]] of a [[id:9f66f38c-1072-4146-9efe-5a90f984d480][continuous]] [[id:87d42439-b03b-48be-84ab-2215b4733dd7][function]] \(f\) on a closed [[id:a995fc0e-d695-4662-a94f-4daad08de069][interval]] \([a,b]\):

1. Find the values of \(f\) at the [[id:f66472e4-0f97-494d-91e0-0e70cd6026ef][critical numbers]] of \(f\) in \((a,b)\).
2. Find the values of \(f\) at the endpoints of the interval.
3. The largest values of Steps 1. and 2. are the absolute maximum values while the smallest values are the aboslute minimum values.
#+end_quote
