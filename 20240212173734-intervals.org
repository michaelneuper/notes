#+title: Interval

Geometrically seen as line segments on $\mathbb{R}$.

- $(a, b)$ means $\{x \in \mathbb{R} | a < x < b \}$
- $[a, b]$ means $\{x \in \mathbb{R} | a \le x \le b \}$
- $(a, \infty)$ means $\{x \in \mathbb{R} | a < x \}$
- $(-\infty, b)$ means $\{x \in \mathbb{R} | x \le b \}$
- $(-\infty, \infty)$ means $\mathbb{R}$
